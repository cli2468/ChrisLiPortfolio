## Design Context

### Users
This portfolio primarily represents Chris himself: a showcase for his websites, frontend craft, and design creativity. It should work as a self-branding piece that Chris can repeatedly share with potential clients as proof of taste, capability, and originality.

Primary use cases:
- Show finished work in a way that feels memorable and premium
- Communicate design taste as clearly as technical ability
- Give potential clients confidence that Chris can build websites that feel custom rather than templated
- Create a strong enough visual impression that people want to reach out

### Brand Personality
Bold, aesthetic, tasteful.

The interface should feel confident and design-led without becoming noisy or gimmicky. It should present Chris as someone with strong visual instincts, creative range, and enough restraint to know when to simplify.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first portfolio with the option to support both dark and light mode later.

Reference sites:
- https://kentokawazoe.com/
- https://edwinle.com/
- https://www.sumairha.com/

The overall direction should feel immersive, polished, and intentional. Typography should carry a lot of the visual identity. Motion can be expressive, but it should support the presentation rather than distract from it.

Avoid:
- Generic template-looking portfolio layouts
- Safe SaaS aesthetics that flatten personality
- Overdecorated interfaces that feel busy instead of refined
- Motion that feels excessive, random, or hard to control

### Design Principles
- Lead with taste: every visual choice should feel curated, not default.
- Make creativity visible: the portfolio should clearly signal design sensitivity, not just technical competence.
- Keep hierarchy sharp: typography, spacing, and contrast should make the work easy to scan and satisfying to read.
- Stay memorable without clutter: bold choices are welcome, but each one should earn its place.
- Build trust through polish: transitions, layout rhythm, and content presentation should feel finished and client-ready.
